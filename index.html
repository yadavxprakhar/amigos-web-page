Who We Are

Driven by Youth, Focused on Grassroots Change

We empower dynamic youth networks to drive bottom-up, grassroots change directly inside underserved communities.

As a registered Section 8 non-profit, InAmigos Foundation acts as a catalyst for sustainable development—mobilizing passionate volunteers to bridge systemic gaps and deliver direct aid where it is needed most.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common inquiries about volunteering, donations, and our operations.

How can I volunteer for a specific project?

You can volunteer by filling out the inquiry form above and selecting your area of interest. Our team will review your application and get in touch to onboard you into the project of your choice.

Are donations tax-exempt under Section 80G?

Yes, InAmigos Foundation is fully registered under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. All financial contributions are eligible for a 50% tax deduction. We will share the official tax exemption certificate directly to your email.

Can college students open local student chapters?

Absolutely! We actively support student-led chapters in colleges and universities. Select "Corporate/Student Partnerships" in the form above, and our youth team will guide you on how to start a chapter in your institution.

How does the foundation ensure fund transparency?

We believe in absolute transparency. We are registered with NITI Aayog Darpan and undergo annual audits. Regular impact reports, including itemized receipts for meals, schooling kits, and animal medical care, are published for our community of sponsors.
